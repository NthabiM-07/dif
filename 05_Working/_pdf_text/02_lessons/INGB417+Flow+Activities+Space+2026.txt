Flow systems in a typical manufacturing facility
• Materials Management system = purchasing
   • Info associated with it include: Production forecasts, demand forecasts, inventory records,
     purchase orders, stock requisitions, bills of lading, receiving reports, move tickets, order
     payments
• Material Flow system = production
   • Info associated with it include: Production schedules, work order releases, bar codes, route
     sheets, assembly charts, warehouse records
• Physical distribution system
   • Info associated with it include: sales orders, packing lists, shipping reports and releases,
     invoices, bills of lading

Design principle is from Bottom Up
      (e.g. from workstation/desk to units/depts to groups/faculties to head
              office/campus)


Space incl. equipment, materials and personnel

Think: workstations, inventory, tools, storage, maintenance, operators,
machines

      Visualization helps – from above, 2D or 3D
Aisle space to promote effective FLOW

• Too narrow will cause congestion
• Too wide is wasted space
• 80-100cm around machines
• Standard specs for people, forklifts, doors, trucks etc.
• Usually calculated as allowance % of netto area
