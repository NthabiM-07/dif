                     Definitions

Facilities design is the organization of a company’s
physical assets (buildings, equipment, materials) to
promote efficient use of resources. Facilities design
therefore includes location, plant/factory layouts
and material handlings systems.

Manufacturing process design includes designing
the products, then designing the required
manufacturing process activities to make these
products. (this may involve several options and
many interrelationships between activities)
                   Responsibilities

Building design is an architect’s job together with
civil engineers.

Manufacturing process design is an (inter-)
discipline specific engineering job,
resulting in the LAYOUT as deliverable. (Layout
means - the physical arrangement of production
machines and equipment, workstations, operators,
materials in all kinds of stages, and the material
handling equipment.)

             1. DEFINE the problem

Ask the right questions e.g.
 •   Ask WHY (re)design facility?
 •   Purpose (or re-) of facility
 •   Align to business goals / objectives
 •   Define Customer/User requirements
 •   Investigate/map current state &
 •   Rules and regulations to adhere to
 •   Ask WHAT-IF’s and other questions
             2. ANALYZE the problem

• Research factors influencing the
  problem, if possible, get to root cause
  (e.g. is space really the problem if
  equipment can change?)
• Identify spaces,
                process flow,
                    equipment
• Gather relevant operational and facility
  data and analyze for insight
                3. Determine DESIGN
                   REQUIREMENTS
• Focus on space and functional needs for all the
  relevant things happening in a facility eg (for
  production factory):

  • Raw materials / parts receiving, storage
  • Production process activities, workstations,
    departments, operators, equipment, material
    handling strategies
  • Work-in-progress storage
  • Finished goods storage, dispatch
  • All administrative and management personnel
    offices
  • Parking for staff, deliveries and customers
  • Security
