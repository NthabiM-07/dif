 INGB417 FACILITIES DESIGN – CLARIFYING OBJECTIVES AND REQUIREMENTS

Facility design objectives and requirements are essential elements in the process of designing a
facility, whether it's a manufacturing plant, warehouse, o ice building, or any other type of
facility. While they are closely related, they serve di erent purposes and are used at di erent
stages of the design process. Here's a breakdown of each:

1. Facility Design Objectives:

 - Facility design objectives outline the overarching goals and aims of the facility design project.
These objectives are broad and strategic, focusing on the desired outcomes of the facility
design process. They typically reﬂect the organization's mission, vision, and long-term strategic
goals.

 - Examples of facility design objectives may include meeting market demand, improving
operational e iciency, enhancing employee productivity and safety, reducing environmental
impact, optimizing space utilization, minimizing construction costs, or ensuring compliance
with regulations and standards.

2. Facility Design Requirements:

  - Facility design requirements are speciﬁc and measurable statements against which a
facility design can be measured to be considered successful. These requirements are derived
from the objectives and serve as the basis for developing the design solutions. They deﬁne the
functional, operational, regulatory, and performance criteria that the facility must satisfy.

 - Requirements can cover various aspects of the facility, including production process design
speciﬁcations, spatial layout, infrastructure, equipment speciﬁcations, systems, environmental
considerations, safety standards, accessibility, and aesthetics.

In summary, facility design objectives set the overarching goals whereas requirements must be
deﬁned such that design options can be evaluated, and when decisions are made this is what is
referenced. Together, they form a structured approach to achieving a well-designed facility that
meets the needs of its users and stakeholders.



For MANUFACTURING facility: some Objectives examples

1. Production Capacity

To establish a manufacturing facility capable of meeting market demand and accommodating
future growth while maintaining operational e iciency and ﬂexibility.

2. Layout and Flow

To design a facility layout that optimizes material ﬂow, minimizes production bottlenecks, and
enhances productivity by streamlining workﬂow and maximizing space utilization.

3. Equipment and Machinery

 To procure and install state-of-the-art equipment and machinery that meet production
requirements, ensure product quality, and promote worker safety while maximizing operational
uptime and minimizing maintenance downtime.

4. Environmental and Regulatory Compliance

To ensure compliance with environmental regulations, zoning requirements, and safety

5. Quality Assurance and Control

To implement robust quality assurance and control measures throughout the production

To optimize energy usage, reduce carbon footprint, and promote sustainability by implementing

2. Layout and Flow:

 - The facility layout should be optimized to minimize material handling distances, streamline
workﬂow and minimize waste.

  - Clear delineation of production ‘zones’ or workareas, storage areas, and support facilities
(e.g., utilities, maintenance, o ices) should be established.

 - Material ﬂow diagrams and process maps should be developed to visualize the entire
process.

 - The production process must ensure e icient movement of raw materials, work-in-progress,
and ﬁnished goods through the production process.

3. Equipment and Machinery:

 - Machinery and equipment must be selected and installed according to the production
process design requirements and industry standards.

 - The facility design must adhere to environmental regulations regarding emissions, waste

 - Compliance with building codes, zoning regulations, and permits for hazardous materials
