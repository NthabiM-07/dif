  What is a facility:
A FACILITY includes all fixed assets to achieve a production/service objective

• Design year = 5-10 years into future
• Implying lots of uncertainty, designers inflate estimates to ‘hedge their bets’
• Major stages in factory include: Supply (purchasing), manufacture
  (production/operations) and distribution (sales)
• Overall flow from raw materials to finished goods = logistics or supply chain
  management system
• Input (receiving) and Output (dispatch) – this can happen for whole facility or
  between ‘departments’ or even workstations (input/pickup/ingress to
  output/delivery/egress)
• Definition of ‘workstation’(3.6.1) vs ‘department’ (3.6.2) definition
 What is an Activity in a facility?
• An activity is a process or part of a process or step in a process
• Not all activities need machines, but all need functional space (e.g. meetings
  where strategy is planned and decisions made)
• Measuring ‘’activities’’ between departments influence design e.g. physical
  material flow or information flow
• challenge is to define what is meant by ‘activity’ and to measure either qualitatively
  or quantitively e.g. nr of movements between, if costly, then more is not effective,
  so aim for less)
• If idea is to minimize cost of flow:
   • Eliminating UNNECESSARY movements
   • Minimizing travel distances
   • Eliminating manual handling
   • Reducing flow density

Definition of SLP
Jacobs & Chase : SLP is a technique for solving layout problems when the use
of numerical flow data between departments is not practical. An activity
relationship chart is adjusted by trial and error until a satisfactory adjacency
pattern is obtained.

Tompkins: SLP is a procedure where block layouts are developed logically and
sequentially following a step by step process and requires a good
understanding of the process flow and relationships between activities.

Input data & Activities
WHAT is produced at each step in the process? (finished goods, assemblies, parts, components, raw
materials needed)

HOW MUCH at each step in the process? (pieces/day, units/hour or minute etc, demand)

Activities understanding needed:

HOW or with WHAT will it be processed? (machines, operations, manual labor)
What is the SEQUENCE, logical steps? (activities in series, parallel, outsourced, produced)
HOW is it moved between steps in the process?
WHEN and HOW LONG does it take?
