 Ch9: Facility auxiliary systems
• These are often architects, civil engineers & builder’s responsibilities
• BUT the facility planner need to know WHAT systems are required WHERE
• Systems to include:
   • Structural (walls, columns, roofs) (9.2)
   • Enclosure (barriers against wind/water/humans incl: walls, windows, floors and for comfort incl:
     doors, ventilation, thermal) (9.3)
   • Atmospheric (temperature incl heating/cooling, humidity, clothing, ‘’clean’’ rooms) (9.4)
   • Electrical (lighting and other systems requiring electricity) (9.5)
   • Life safety (fire protection, seismic events and power failures) (9.6)
   • Sanitation (water, plumbing) (9.7)

• The idea of Automation (think SMART)
• Sometimes Maintenance functions are also seen as part of auxiliary systems
