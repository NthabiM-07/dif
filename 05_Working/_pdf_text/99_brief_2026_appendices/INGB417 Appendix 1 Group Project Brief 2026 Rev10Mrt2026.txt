                      Facilities Design: Group Project brief
    (manufacturing 1 finished product, complex production processes involving

Hypnos Mattress Manufacturers is a leading South African producer of premium-quality mattresses. A
recent increase in market demand left the company unable to fulfil production requirements with their
current strategy. The board of directors decided to develop a new facility specifically for the company’s
double-size pocket spring mattress, thereby freeing up production capacity for their other product lines
in the current facility. At the new facility the production processes have to be redesigned to meet the
increased demand. Current demand projections are estimated at 200 000 mattresses per year. The
management team appointed a facility design team to develop a detailed facility plan to aid with the
strategic decision of where the new facility should be located.

To maintain their competitive edge and signature mattress style, Hypnos Mattress Manufacturers
produces its own polyurethane foam with a unique chemical structure for the internal cushioning layers
on-site, rather than relying on external suppliers for this key component of their mattresses. The core of
the pocket spring mattress consists of wire springs wrapped in a lightweight non-woven polypropylene
fabric, which is secured in place through ultrasonic or thermal welding of the fabric. The additional
cushioning and protective layers of the mattress are then added and sewn into place before packaging
the mattresses for distribution to customers. The company’s current production process can be seen in
the following video:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S2UjKJCQlxc

The design team must use the production process illustrated in this video as a base for the production
process design used in the new facility, with adjustments and upgraded material handling methods as
required to develop the new facility.

Manufacturing and management policies to be considered when designing facilities:


   1. Manufacturing Operating Times:
      The company operates for one 9-hour shift per day, 5 days a week. Operators receive a 30-
      minute lunch break and two 15-minute tea breaks per day. A total of 220 working days per year
      can be assumed. Overtime or a Saturday shift is possible, but management would prefer to
      avoid it where possible, unless flexible capacity is required for unforeseen spikes in demand.

   2. Inventory Levels:
      The company’s inventory policy stipulates that 5 days’ worth of raw materials must be kept in
      stock due to long supplier lead times. Finished goods storage must be sufficient for 2 weeks of
      stock, as the Hypnos Mattress Manufacturers has an international customer footprint and needs
      to ship large batches at a time. The Work-in-Progress inventory levels and space allocation must
      be determined by the design team.

   3. Deliveries and Collections:
      The facility must allow space for small order collection on-site by customers and the dispatch of
      large orders from the facility. Hypnos Mattress Manufacturers has its own fleet of delivery
      trucks serving the current facility, but the fleet will not have sufficient capacity to transport the
      new facility’s products as well. The design team must advise the management on a suitable
      delivery protocol for bulk orders from the new facility. Also advise management on a raw
      material delivery schedule dependent on the facility warehousing capacity. The finished goods
      dispatch schedule must also be recommended based on the design of the facility and the
      delivery protocol.

Management policies:

   •    Raw material receival and storage/warehouse areas, material and component staging areas
        during manufacturing and assembly, as well as Finished goods storage/warehouse and staging
        areas must be clearly indicated.
    •   Clear indication on entry and exit to the premises must be visible on the layout.
    •   Parking must be provided for all personnel. Personnel include management, administration and
        operators. Reasoning and calculations for determining parking space must be provided.
    •   Lockers for plant personnel are provided as well as a cafeteria providing basic food, but no
        Health services are provided. There should be enough Rest Rooms provided in both offices as
        well as in the Manufacturing areas, as per standards.
    •   Maintenance and Spare parts storage rooms need to be available in the facility, although the
        layout detail of those do not have to be done.
    •   Office space must be allocated to the Senior Plant Manager, Operations Manager, Inventory
        Manager, the administrative team, including Reception (for customers and suppliers visiting the
        offices), Purchasing, Sales, Finance, Marketing and Human Resources employees, and the plant
