1. Executive Summary
This report presents the comprehensive design of a new manufacturing facility for TIPTOP
TABLES, which has a need for a new manufacturing plant for only their outdoor table 3-in-1 set.
The project involved identifying the machinery and equipment required for the production of the
company’s product, as well as planning and designing each workstation. In addition, appropriate
material handling and storage strategies were chosen.

Three alternative facility layouts were developed and assessed based on the design objectives
and requirements, with the most suitable option selected for further refinement into the final
master facility plan. The business case for the new facility was also carefully examined to
determine the cost, risk mitigations and schedule.



2. Problem Background
TIPTOP TABLES has been a manufacturer of outdoor tables and chairs in the South African
market for the past 25 years. Among its product offerings, the company has recently experienced
an increase in demand for its versatile 2-in-1 lightweight aluminium outdoor table set, which
includes one table, designed to expand into a larger table if needed, and four matching chairs.

Current forecasts suggest a demand of up to 5 000 sets per month for this specific product line.
However, TIPTOP TABLES' existing manufacturing facility is unable to meet this demand, as it is
also used for the production of various other products. To address this challenge, management
has identified the need for a new facility dedicated solely to the production of this outdoor set.


3. Design Objectives
The following design objectives, categorised under their relevant headings, have been
established to ensure the successful design of TIPTOP TABLES' new manufacturing facility:
➢ Production:
    To design a manufacturing facility capable of meeting the projected monthly demand of
    5000 aluminium outdoor sets, with built-in flexibility for future expansion.
➢   Site:
    To design and recommend a site that has access from public roads for all delivery and
    collection vehicles, complies with zoning and industrial safety regulations, and
    accommodates both current and future operational requirements.

➢   Layout and Flow:
    To develop a layout that minimises material handling and unnecessary movement of
    operators and goods, reduces bottlenecks, and has a logical flow of materials, from
    receiving to shipping, on a greenfield site.
➢   Equipment and Machinery:
    To select suitable machinery and equipment specifically for cutting, welding, grinding,
    powder coating, assembly, and packaging aluminium components that meet throughput
    requirements, ensure product quality, prioritise worker safety, and minimise downtime.

4. Design Requirements
The following design requirements, categorised under their relevant headings, have been
established to ensure the successful design of TIPTOP TABLES' new manufacturing facility:
➢ Production:
    The facility shall:
        o Produce a minimum of 5 000 aluminium outdoor sets per month.
        o Stock enough raw materials to support two weeks of production.
        o A 5% scrap rate must be factored in during the cutting stage of production.
➢ Site:
  The facility shall:
      o Be accessible to public roads for delivery and collection.
      o Allow space for truck deliveries and customer pickups.
      o Store all raw materials and components indoors.
      o Provide a designated collection point for customer orders.
      o Allocate 20% of the floor area for future expansion.
      o Include a separate enclosed welding area, considering safety and ventilation.
      o Provide robot safety zones around the automated welding stations.

➢ Layout and Flow:
  The facility shall:
     o Provide clear paths for material and operator movement between processes.
     o Include vehicle access routes for incoming and outgoing goods.
     o Allow for work-in-progress (WIP) storage areas between operations.
     o Accommodate stacking and temporary storage of outsourced chairs awaiting
         assembly.


➢ Specific Design Requirements
  The facility shall adhere to the following specific requirements set by TIPTOP TABLES:

       o   Welded WIP shall have access to outside for the onloading of chairs.
       o   Assembly WIP shall have access to the outside for the offloading of chairs.
       o   Raw materials shall not be close to the automated welding operation.
       o   The cutting process shall not be close to the automated welding operation
       o   The WIP of cut parts shall not be close to the automated welding operation.
       o   Manual welding shall not be close to the powder coating operation.
       o   Manual welding shall not be close to the assembly operation.
       o   Manual welding shall not be close to the packing operation.
       o   Manual welding shall not be close to the finished goods storage.
       o   Raw materials shall be close to the cutting operation.
       o   The cutting operation shall be close to the WIP of cut parts.
       o   Automated and manual welding shall be close to each other.
       o   WIP of structures and tabletops shall be close to each other.
       o   Packaging and finished goods shall be close to each other.

8. Evaluating Alternatives
To determine the most suitable facility layout, we employed the Weighted Factor Comparison
method. This technique evaluates each alternative based on the four topics stated in the design
requirements, each assigned a weight according to its level of importance. Not all factors carry
the same significance, so weights were allocated on a priority scale from 1 to 10, where:
    • 1–4: Less critical
    • 5–7: Important
    • 8–10: Highly important
Each alternative was then rated on a performance scale from 1 to 5, where 5 represents the most
favourable outcome for that factor. The table below summarises the evaluation:

Table 29: Weighted Factor Comparison Evaluation

Alternative 1 lack designated access for the onloading and offloading of chairs, which is a key
requirement. Additionally, the welding operation is not separated as specified by TIPTOP TABLES.
While Alternative 3 does provide a separate welding area, the overall material flow is inefficient
and impractical.

Alternative 2, however, includes a dedicated access point for loading and unloading chairs, a
separate welding section, and meets all of the specific design criteria set by TIPTOP TABLES.

Given its stronger performance - particularly in the most critical areas - Alternative 2 is the
preferred layout option.

11. Final Recommendations and Remarks
I recommend that TIPTOP TABLES consider purchasing a plot of land approximately 1 hectare in
size, or just under, as a suitable site for the construction of the new manufacturing facility.

A final remark would be to investigate the reasoning behind the current preference to keep the
WIP of cut parts away from the welding area. From a production flow perspective, positioning
these processes in closer proximity would create a more logical and streamlined workflow,
potentially improving overall efficiency.
